# GDPR and Data Privacy Compliance Guide

**Version:** 2.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Owner:** Legal & Compliance
**Review Cycle:** Annual

## 1. Overview

InsightHub collects and processes personal data about customers, employees, and prospects. This guide explains how the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) applies to our operations, the rights of data subjects, and the obligations of all staff who handle personal data.

GDPR applies to all processing of personal data relating to EU and EEA residents, regardless of where InsightHub is located.

## 2. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Personal Data** | Any information that can identify a living individual (name, email, IP address, customer ID) |
| **Processing** | Any operation on personal data: collection, storage, use, sharing, deletion |
| **Data Controller** | InsightHub — determines why and how data is processed |
| **Data Processor** | Third parties processing data on InsightHub's behalf (e.g., payment gateways, cloud providers) |
| **Data Subject** | The individual whose personal data is processed |
| **DPA** | Data Processing Agreement — required contract with all processors |

## 3. Lawful Bases for Processing

InsightHub must have a valid lawful basis for every processing activity. The six bases under GDPR Article 6 are:

1. **Consent** — Data subject has freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent (used for: marketing emails, cookies)
2. **Contract** — Processing is necessary to perform a contract with the data subject (used for: order fulfilment, account management)
3. **Legal Obligation** — Processing is required by law (used for: tax reporting, AML checks)
4. **Vital Interests** — Rare; applies in life-or-death situations
5. **Public Task** — Not applicable to InsightHub's operations
6. **Legitimate Interests** — Processing is necessary for InsightHub's legitimate interests, balanced against individual rights (used for: fraud prevention, system security, analytics)

The lawful basis for each processing activity is documented in the **Record of Processing Activities (RoPA)**, maintained by Legal.

## 4. Data Subject Rights

InsightHub must respond to data subject rights requests within **30 calendar days** (extendable to 3 months for complex cases, with notification):

| Right | What It Means | How to Submit |
|---|---|---|
| Access (DSAR) | Individual can request a copy of all their data | privacy@insighthub.com |
| Rectification | Correct inaccurate data | Privacy portal or customer support |
| Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten") | Delete data where no overriding legal basis exists | privacy@insighthub.com |
| Restriction | Limit processing while a dispute is resolved | privacy@insighthub.com |
| Portability | Receive data in a machine-readable format | Privacy portal |
| Objection | Object to processing based on legitimate interests | privacy@insighthub.com |
| Automated Decision-Making | Request human review of automated decisions | Legal review required |

All DSAR requests must be logged immediately in the Privacy Tracker (Confluence) and escalated to Legal if they involve Restricted data.

## 5. Data Breach Response

Under GDPR Article 33, InsightHub must notify the lead supervisory authority within **72 hours** of becoming aware of a breach that poses a risk to individuals' rights and freedoms.

**Internal escalation process:**

1. Employee discovers or suspects a breach → immediately notify IT Security (security@insighthub.com) and Legal (legal@insighthub.com)
2. IT Security contains the incident and assesses scope within 4 hours
3. Legal assesses notification obligation within 24 hours
4. If notification required, Legal submits the supervisory authority report within 72 hours
5. Affected individuals are notified without undue delay if the breach creates high risk to them

Do not attempt to investigate or contain a breach without IT Security's involvement.

## 6. Privacy by Design

New systems, products, or processes that involve personal data must complete a **Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA)** before implementation. The PIA template is in Confluence. Legal must review and approve PIAs for:

- Any new data collection from customers or employees
- New third-party integrations that receive personal data
- Changes to data retention practices

## 7. International Data Transfers

Personal data of EU/EEA residents must not be transferred to countries without an adequacy decision unless a valid transfer mechanism is in place:

- **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs):** Must be incorporated into contracts with all non-EEA processors
- **Binding Corporate Rules:** Applicable for intra-group transfers
- **Adequacy Decision:** Countries approved by the EU Commission (e.g., UK post-Brexit adequacy, Canada PIPEDA)

Legal maintains the International Transfer Register.

## 8. Data Processor Management

Before engaging any third-party processor:

1. Complete the Vendor Risk Assessment (IT Security)
2. Confirm the vendor's GDPR compliance and DPA availability
3. Legal signs the DPA before any data is shared
4. The vendor is added to the Processor Register

## 9. Consent Management

Marketing consent must be:
- **Freely given** — no forced consent bundled with a service
- **Specific** — each type of marketing listed separately
- **Informed** — clear privacy notice at point of collection
- **Unambiguous** — affirmative action (tick box); pre-ticked boxes are invalid

Consent records must be retained for a minimum of **3 years after consent is withdrawn** for audit purposes.
