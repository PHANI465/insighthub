# Performance Review Process

**Document Type:** HR Policy
**Version:** 2.4
**Effective Date:** October 1, 2024
**Owner:** People Operations
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Overview

InsightHub uses a structured annual performance review cycle to align individual contributions with company objectives, support career development, and inform compensation decisions. All permanent employees who have completed their 90-day probationary period participate.

## 2. Review Calendar

| Milestone | Timing |
|---|---|
| Goal-setting (new cycle) | January 15 – February 15 |
| Mid-year check-in | June 1 – June 30 |
| Year-end self-assessment opens | October 1 |
| 360 feedback window | October 1 – October 21 |
| Manager review submissions due | November 7 |
| Calibration sessions | November 11 – November 22 |
| Results communicated to employees | December 1 – December 15 |
| Merit increase effective date | January 1 (following year) |

## 3. Rating Scale

Performance is assessed on a 5-point scale:

| Rating | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Distinguished | Consistently exceeds all goals; role model for others; exceptional impact |
| 4 | Exceeds Expectations | Exceeds most goals; delivers beyond the scope of the role |
| 3 | Meets Expectations | Achieves all agreed goals; fully competent at required level |
| 2 | Developing | Partially meets goals; clear development plan in place |
| 1 | Unsatisfactory | Fails to meet goals; performance improvement plan required |

A rating of 2 or below triggers a mandatory Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) within 14 days.

## 4. Goal Framework (OKRs)

Each employee agrees **3–5 Objectives** with their manager during January goal-setting. Each objective contains **2–4 Key Results** (KRs) that are:

- **Specific** — clearly define what success looks like
- **Measurable** — include a numeric or verifiable indicator
- **Time-bound** — anchored to dates within the review year
- **Aligned** — traceable to team and company OKRs

Goals are recorded in Workday and can be updated by mutual agreement during the mid-year check-in.

## 5. Mid-Year Check-In

The mid-year check-in (June) is a **conversation, not a rating**. Managers and employees discuss:

- Progress against each KR (on track, at risk, or blocked)
- Any changes to priorities since January
- Development needs for H2
- Any barriers the manager can remove

A brief written summary of the check-in is logged in Workday.

## 6. 360 Feedback

Employees nominate **3–6 peers** for 360 feedback. Managers may add reviewers. Feedback is:

- Anonymous to the employee (aggregated, minimum 3 responses before shown)
- Used as supporting context, not a primary rating input
- Provided via the Culture Amp platform

## 7. Calibration

After manager reviews are submitted, HR facilitates calibration sessions by department to ensure:

- Ratings are consistent across teams
- Distribution roughly follows a bell curve (fewer than 15% Exceeds/Distinguished, fewer than 10% Developing/Unsatisfactory)
- Decisions are free from bias

Calibration outcomes take precedence over individual manager ratings where inconsistency is identified.

## 8. Compensation Link

| Rating | Merit Increase Range |
|---|---|
| Distinguished (5) | 8 – 12% |
| Exceeds (4) | 5 – 8% |
| Meets (3) | 2 – 4% |
| Developing (2) | 0% |
| Unsatisfactory (1) | 0% (PIP review) |

Merit increases are subject to budget approval and market benchmarking. They do not constitute a guaranteed entitlement.

## 9. Probationary Reviews

New employees undergo a **30-day, 60-day, and 90-day check-in** during probation. These are informal conversations between the employee and manager; no formal rating is assigned. At 90 days the manager confirms either successful completion of probation or extension.

## 10. Promotion Criteria

Promotions are considered separately from merit increases. An employee is eligible for promotion when they have:

- Sustained a rating of Exceeds (4) or Distinguished (5) for two consecutive years
- Demonstrated competencies at the next level
- A vacancy or business case at the target level
