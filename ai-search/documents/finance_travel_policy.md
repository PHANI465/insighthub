# Business Travel Policy

**Document Type:** Finance Policy
**Version:** 3.3
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Owner:** Finance Operations
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes standards and limits for InsightHub business travel to ensure cost-effective travel while maintaining employee safety, comfort, and productivity.

## 2. Travel Approval

All business travel requires advance approval:

- **Domestic travel:** Manager approval at least **5 business days** before departure.
- **International travel:** VP approval at least **15 business days** before departure.
- Emergency travel (e.g., critical customer escalation) may be approved retroactively with documented justification.

Do not purchase travel before receiving written approval in Workday.

## 3. Booking Requirements

All travel must be booked through **Concur Travel** using the company travel portal. Direct bookings with airlines or hotels should only be made if:

- The required itinerary is unavailable in Concur, or
- The Concur price exceeds the best-available external price by more than 10%.

In either case, the booking must be documented in the Concur expense claim.

**Advance booking:** Book flights and hotels at least **14 days in advance** to minimise cost. Bookings within 7 days require manager justification in the Concur notes field.

## 4. Air Travel

| Journey Type | Permitted Class |
|---|---|
| Domestic, under 3 hours | Economy |
| Domestic, over 3 hours | Economy (Premium Economy with Director+ approval) |
| International, under 6 hours | Economy |
| International, 6+ hours | Business Class (requires VP approval) |

**Lowest logical airfare:** Always select the lowest-cost itinerary that arrives at the destination with at least 60 minutes before the first commitment. Red-eye flights are not mandatory but are encouraged for long-haul trips to save hotel costs.

**Seat upgrades:** Personal upgrades using miles or points are the employee's own expense. Paid seat upgrades are not reimbursable.

## 5. Hotels

| Location | Nightly Rate Cap |
|---|---|
| Tier 1 cities (NYC, SF, London, Singapore) | $350 |
| Other domestic cities | $250 |
| Other international cities | $300 |

Hotels must be within reasonable proximity to the business destination. Loyalty programme points from company-paid stays belong to the employee. Expenses for in-room dining are capped at the meal per-diem rate.

## 6. Ground Transportation

| Option | Policy |
|---|---|
| Rideshare (Uber, Lyft) | Preferred for airport transfers and short trips |
| Taxis | Acceptable; obtain a receipt |
| Rental cars | Economy or compact class; GPS and damage waiver included |
| Personal vehicle | Reimbursed at $0.67/mile (IRS rate); log via Concur |
| Public transit | Fully reimbursable; keep receipts |

Rental cars require the employee to be on the company's insurance rider (confirm with Finance before renting). Personal vehicles must be adequately insured by the employee.

## 7. Per Diem Rates

When meals are not covered by a conference or client host, per diem rates apply:

| Location | Daily Meal and Incidentals |
|---|---|
| Domestic USA | $75/day |
| Canada, Mexico | $100/day |
| Western Europe | $130/day |
| Asia Pacific | $120/day |
| Other international | $150/day |

Per diems are paid as a flat rate; receipts are not required when using per diem rates. Employees may not claim both per diem and individual meal receipts for the same day.

## 8. Extended Trips and Personal Days

Employees may extend a business trip for personal travel provided:

- The personal portion of flights and hotels is paid entirely by the employee.
- Business and personal costs are clearly separated in Concur.
- The extended stay does not create additional company costs.

## 9. Travel Safety

For international travel to higher-risk destinations:

- Employees must register their trip with HR and share their itinerary.
- InsightHub maintains a travel assistance programme through International SOS (iSOS). The member number is on the back of the company ID card.
- IT Security must approve connectivity to company systems if traveling to certain restricted countries.

## 10. Unused Tickets and Cancellations

Unused, refundable tickets must be cancelled and refunded. Non-refundable tickets should be retained as travel credits for future business use, not personal use. Travel credits are the company's asset.
