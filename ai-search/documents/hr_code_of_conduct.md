# Code of Conduct

**Document Type:** HR Policy
**Version:** 2.1
**Effective Date:** March 1, 2025
**Owner:** People Operations & Legal
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Purpose

InsightHub expects every employee, contractor, and business partner to act with integrity, professionalism, and respect. This Code of Conduct defines the standards of behaviour that uphold our reputation and legal obligations.

## 2. Core Values

Our Code of Conduct is anchored in four values:

- **Integrity** — Act honestly and transparently in all dealings.
- **Collaboration** — Treat colleagues, customers, and partners with respect.
- **Innovation** — Pursue creative solutions while managing risk responsibly.
- **Accountability** — Take ownership of decisions and their consequences.

## 3. Workplace Respect and Anti-Harassment

InsightHub operates a **zero-tolerance policy** for harassment, discrimination, and bullying in any form, including:

- Verbal abuse, offensive jokes, or derogatory language
- Physical intimidation or unwanted contact
- Discrimination based on race, gender, age, disability, religion, sexual orientation, national origin, or any other protected characteristic
- Cyberbullying via company or personal channels when directed at colleagues

Any employee who experiences or witnesses such behaviour must report it immediately via the Ethics Hotline (available 24/7 at 1-800-555-0199) or directly to HR. All reports will be treated confidentially and investigated within 5 business days.

## 4. Conflicts of Interest

Employees must disclose any situation that creates, or could be perceived to create, a conflict between personal interests and InsightHub's interests. Examples include:

- Ownership of a financial interest in a competitor or supplier
- Employment or board membership at a competitor
- Personal relationships with direct reports or vendor contacts
- Receiving gifts valued above $75 from vendors or customers

Disclosures must be made in writing to HR and the relevant VP within 5 business days of the conflict arising.

## 5. Confidentiality and Data Protection

All employees handle confidential information. This includes customer data, financial projections, product roadmaps, employee records, and proprietary algorithms. Employees must:

- Not disclose confidential information to unauthorised persons, including family members.
- Use company data only for legitimate business purposes.
- Follow the Information Security Policy when handling, storing, or transmitting data.
- Report any suspected data breach immediately to the IT Security team.

Confidentiality obligations survive termination of employment for a period of **3 years**.

## 6. Social Media and Public Communications

Employees may maintain personal social media accounts provided they:

- Clearly indicate that opinions expressed are their own, not InsightHub's.
- Do not disclose confidential business information.
- Do not make disparaging statements about the company, colleagues, or customers.
- Obtain PR approval before posting company-related content on public channels.

Only designated spokespersons may make official statements on behalf of InsightHub.

## 7. Anti-Corruption and Gifts Policy

InsightHub prohibits bribery and corrupt practices in any jurisdiction. Employees must not offer, pay, request, or accept any item of value intended to influence a business decision. Gifts received from external parties that exceed $75 in value must be declared to the Finance team.

## 8. Use of Company Resources

Company assets — including IT equipment, office facilities, and budget — are for business use. Incidental personal use is permitted provided it does not:

- Involve excessive time or bandwidth
- Create legal, reputational, or security risks
- Result in additional cost to the company

## 9. Reporting Violations

Employees who suspect a violation of this Code should report it through one of the following channels:

1. **Ethics Hotline (anonymous):** 1-800-555-0199
2. **HR direct:** ethics@insighthub.com
3. **Line Manager or VP**

Retaliation against any person who makes a good-faith report is itself a violation of this Code and will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

## 10. Consequences of Violations

Violations may result in disciplinary action ranging from a formal written warning to termination, depending on severity. Legal action may also be pursued where applicable.
