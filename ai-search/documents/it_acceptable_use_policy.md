# Acceptable Use Policy

**Document Type:** IT Policy
**Version:** 3.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Owner:** IT Security
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) governs how InsightHub employees and contractors may use company-owned or company-managed technology resources. The goal is to protect company data, ensure legal compliance, and maintain a productive and respectful digital environment.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all use of:

- Company-issued devices (laptops, mobile phones, tablets)
- Company network infrastructure (office Wi-Fi, VPN)
- Company-licensed software and SaaS applications
- Company email accounts and communication platforms
- Cloud services accessed using company credentials

## 3. Permitted Personal Use

Incidental personal use of company IT resources is permitted provided it:

- Occurs outside core working hours where possible
- Does not consume significant bandwidth or storage (streaming video for personal use is prohibited during office hours)
- Does not involve illegal, harmful, or offensive content
- Does not introduce security risks (e.g., installing unapproved software)

## 4. Prohibited Activities

The following activities are strictly prohibited on company systems or networks:

### 4.1 Illegal Activities
- Accessing, downloading, or distributing copyrighted material without a valid licence
- Accessing or distributing material that is illegal in the jurisdiction of use
- Conducting or facilitating any form of fraud, harassment, or unauthorised access to external systems

### 4.2 Security Risks
- Installing software not approved by IT (see Software Procurement Policy)
- Disabling antivirus, firewall, or MDM controls
- Cryptocurrency mining using company resources
- Sharing login credentials with any other person, including colleagues

### 4.3 Competitive and Reputational Risks
- Soliciting InsightHub customers or employees on behalf of a competitor
- Disclosing confidential company information via personal accounts
- Using company systems to operate a personal business

### 4.4 Inappropriate Content
- Viewing, creating, or distributing sexually explicit, violent, or discriminatory content
- Sending offensive, threatening, or harassing communications to colleagues or external parties

## 5. Email and Communication Standards

Company email is a business tool. Employees should:

- Use professional language in all written communications
- Not use company email to subscribe to personal mailing lists or commercial newsletters
- Exercise caution before clicking links or opening attachments from unknown senders
- Report suspicious emails to IT Security immediately at security@insighthub.com

Phishing simulation emails are sent periodically by IT Security. Clicking a simulated phishing link triggers mandatory retraining.

## 6. Cloud Storage and File Sharing

Approved cloud storage services: **Microsoft OneDrive** and **SharePoint**.

Unapproved services (e.g., personal Google Drive, Dropbox, iCloud) must not be used to store or transfer company data. External file sharing must use company-approved channels (SharePoint share links) with appropriate access controls.

## 7. Software Installation

Employees must not install software on company devices without IT approval. To request new software, submit a ticket via the IT Service Desk. See the **Software Procurement Policy** for the full approval process and timelines.

## 8. Monitoring

InsightHub reserves the right to monitor all activity on company-owned systems, networks, and accounts to:

- Ensure compliance with this policy
- Investigate suspected security incidents
- Meet legal and regulatory obligations

Monitoring is conducted in accordance with applicable privacy laws. Employees have no expectation of privacy when using company IT resources.

## 9. Social Media from Company Devices

Social media access on company devices is permitted during breaks for personal use. Employees must not post company-confidential information or make statements that could be mistaken for official company communications. See the **Code of Conduct** for social media guidelines.

## 10. Consequences

Violations of this policy will be investigated and may result in:

- Formal written warning
- Suspension of IT access privileges
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination
- Referral to law enforcement for criminal violations
